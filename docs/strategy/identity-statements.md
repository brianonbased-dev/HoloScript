# Identity statements (major components)

**Audience:** GTM, partnerships, onboarding — *what problem we remove*, not which libraries we use.  
**Rule:** Lead with the outcome and who it is for; technology is supporting detail only.  
**Source task:** blackberry-trap platform strategy — “problem solved, not technology used.”

---

## Core (`@holoscript/core` and language)

**We remove** the need for teams to hand-wire physics, networking, assets, and tooling as separate islands just to ship one spatial experience.  
**We replace it with** a single declarative source of truth for scenes and behaviors so builds, tests, and targets stay aligned as the product grows.  
**We are not** “another generic game engine” pitch; we are the **semantic layer** that keeps 3D/XR intent portable, reviewable, and automatable.

---

## Studio (HoloScript Studio)

**We remove** the context-switch tax between authoring, preview, integrations, and team coordination.  
**We replace it with** an IDE-shaped workspace where the same project connects to repos, deploy targets, and agents without leaving the creative loop.  
**We are not** a slide deck about “the metaverse”; we are **where a builder gets from idea to running scene** with guardrails and integrations in one place.

---

## HoloClaw (agent / daemon layer)

**We remove** the gap between “we have automation ideas” and “something actually runs on a schedule with accountability.”  
**We replace it with** daemon-style agents attached to team workspaces — monitoring, proposing actions, and executing approved skills against real repos and services.  
**We are not** a generic chatbot bolt-on; we are **operational agents** with a home in the team’s board and budgets.

---

## MCP (Model Context Protocol surface)

**We remove** the integration maze where every AI client reinvents tool discovery, auth, and routing for HoloScript and its connectors.  
**We replace it with** a **standard tool mesh** so models and hosts can call compilation, absorb, connectors, and orchestration the same way — observable and governable.  
**We are not** “yet another proprietary API”; we are **MCP-native** so the ecosystem can compose safely.

---

## HoloLand (spatial / world product)

**We remove** the problem of spatial collaboration feeling like a demo that cannot carry real workflows.  
**We replace it with** worlds where teams **see status, motivation, and integrations in space** — not only flat dashboards — so spatial context matches operational reality.  
**We are not** a single static venue; we are **living environments** tied to the same data and agents the rest of the stack uses.

---

## How to use this doc

- Prefer lifting **one sentence** from each block for decks; keep the **problem line** first.  
- When copy hardcodes stack names (React, Three.js, WASM), move them to a footnote unless the reader is technical.  
- Revisit after major releases — identities track **buyer problems**, not feature lists.
