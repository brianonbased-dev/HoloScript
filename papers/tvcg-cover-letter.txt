Cover Letter
IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics

Dear Editor-in-Chief,

We are resubmitting the manuscript "Trust by Construction: A Simulation Contract
for Reproducible Computational Experiments" as a new submission to IEEE TVCG.
This manuscript was previously submitted (reference TVCG-2026-04-0403) and
returned before external review with three readiness concerns raised by the
Associate Editor. We have addressed each concern in full.

Concern 1 — Individual solution elements need more explanation and justification.
Each of the six simulation contract guarantees now includes an explicit "Why this
shape" discussion that (a) states the design rationale, (b) explains why traditional
export pipelines fail to provide the same property, and (c) explains why in-situ
co-processing approaches also fall short. These additions appear in Section 3.2
(Guarantees 1-6) and are new relative to the prior submission.

Concern 2 — Manuscript errors made some parts unreadable.
The prior submission contained a bibliography migration regression that produced
approximately 30 undefined-citation warnings throughout the compiled PDF, causing
reference markers to appear as "[?]" in the text. This has been corrected: the
bibliography is now an inline thebibliography environment with all 30 cited keys
explicitly defined. The manuscript compiles to zero warnings.

Concern 3 — Several references were incorrect or incorrectly referred to in the text.
All citations have been audited. The manuscript cites 30 unique keys; all 30 have
correct, complete bibitem entries. No orphan entries exist. The bibliography entries
for ASME V&V standards, NAFEMS, Roache GCI, and Zienkiewicz-Zhu SPR have been
verified against their primary sources.

The scientific contribution — embodied simulation, the six-guarantee simulation
contract, and the provenance semiring formalization — is unchanged. The evaluation
results, including TET10 exact solution, TET4 convergence at p = 1.44, and contract
overhead indistinguishable from runtime scheduling variance, are unchanged and
honestly reported, including the explicit acknowledgment of the open NAFEMS LE1
curved-boundary gap.

We confirm this manuscript is submitted solely to IEEE TVCG and is not under
review elsewhere.
